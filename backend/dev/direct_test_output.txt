**Computational‑Biology (Genomics & Computational Biology) – Upper‑Level Elective Requirement**

The biology major with the **Genomics & Computational Biology specialization** requires **18 credit hours of advanced (3000‑level or above) biology coursework**.  These 18 credits are the “upper‑level electives” that go beyond the 40‑unit core (the core includes the 1000‑/2000‑level biology, chemistry, physics, and math courses).  

Below is exactly what counts toward those 18 units:

| Category | What can be used | Typical examples (non‑exhaustive) |
|----------|------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Any BIOL course numbered 3000‑4999** (except the courses specifically excluded) | • BIOL 3240, 3340, 3371, 3481, 3490, 4240, 4242, 4345, 4492, 4510 (Area A) <br>• BIOL 3057, 3151, 3280, 3411, 3421, 3422, 3424, 4026, 4030, 4071, 4072, 4381 (Area B) <br>• BIOL 3220, 3221, 3470, 3494, 3501, 3700, 3810, 4181, 4182, 4183, 4195, 4197, 4720 (Area C) | 3–4 units each |
| **Cross‑listed courses from other departments that count as “advanced biology”** | • PHYSICS 3354 (Introduction to Biological Physics) <br>• PHYSICS 3324 (Biophysics) <br>• ANTHRO 4581 (Principles of Human Anatomy & Development) <br>• CHEM 4810 (General Biochemistry I) <br>• CHEM 4820 (General Biochemistry II) <br>• CHEM 4833 (Biochemistry of Gene Expression) | 3 units each |
| **Independent research (up to 6 units total)** | • BIOL 4950, 4951, 4952, 4953, 4954 (Advanced Independent Study in Biology) | 1–3 units each (max 6) |
| **Biology seminars (up to 3 units total)** | • Any BIOL 49XX seminar course | 1–3 units each (max 3) |

### Rules that apply to the 18‑unit block
* **Exclusions:** The following **may not** be counted toward the 18 units:  
  * BIOL 3030, BIOL 3430, BIOL 3990, BIOL 3991, BIOL 3900, BIOL 4990, BIOL 4991  
  * Courses offered through the School of Continuing & Professional Studies  
  * More than **3 units** of history‑of‑science courses  
* **Grade requirement:** All courses counted toward the major must be taken for a letter grade (when a letter grade is offered) and a grade of **C‑ or better** is required.  
* **Area distribution:** While not “electives” per se, students **must also take at least one course from each of the three areas (A, B, C)** listed above. Those area courses are part of the 18‑unit total.

### Summary
- **Minimum number of upper‑level elective credits:** **18 units** (3000‑level or above).  
- **What satisfies the requirement:** any 3000‑/4000‑level BIOL course (excluding the prohibited ones), plus the six cross‑listed courses (PHYSICS 3354, 3324; ANTHRO 4581; CHEM 4810, 4820, 4833). Up to 6 units of BIOL 4950‑4954 independent research and up to 3 units of BIOL 49XX seminars may be included.  
- **Additional mandatory distribution:** at least one course from each of Areas A, B, C (these also count toward the 18‑unit total).  

So, for the computational‑biology major, the student needs **18 credits of advanced biology coursework** drawn from the above options, with the listed restrictions.
